Vinted Case Study

Displaying data easier for users

Context

As a vintage lover I became a Vinted heavy user for the past two years. Not only buying cool pieces but I did also start selling quite a few.
At this point, I advertised my profile, which consist of a broader exposure of your profile (and pieces for sale) to other users in feed and catalog for seven euros per week. Actually it does work smoothly and sellers increase their sales considerably.

Problem

When users pays for this promotion, they will be able to see statistics: data shows how the promotion is performing. This is quite useful to see if it is really worth it. Basically you are going to see how many views your profile has received, clicks on pieces, new followers and new favorited pieces.
I found the graphic would be way useful if it showed how many views the profile received on the former days without promo. I am fully aware that this would not be the most convenient business decision but the information could be given more briefly in this case.

Solution

In order that the information displayed was useful and intuitive for the user, I have redesigned the statistics section. First of all, the line chart was not convenient to click on points, so I translated it into a bar chart, easier to click on them. Then I added a switch button where users can see statistics based with and without promotion, being the latter way briefer than the former.

Below, the user will find a couple of statements as an overview of the day and then a ranking of the most viewed and linked articles. In this part I did two variations one based on Vinted's classic product grid and the second which is more on the listing side.
